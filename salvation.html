Why Jesus Christ Came?

Redemption

Jesus Christ came to become the lamb that would take away the sins of men.

All throughout the Old Testament, God revealed His plan of redemption. In those times, people atoned for their sins by offering a spotless lamb as a sacrifice. Since the consequence of sin is death, the only way for a sinner to be pardoned was for an innocent and blameless substitute to take their place, paying the price through death‚Äîthis is the concept of atonement.

In the New Testament, Jesus became the ultimate, blameless Lamb‚Äîthe sacrifice God Himself provided for humanity. His atonement is available to all who believe in His name and the finished work of the cross (1 Peter 1:19-20, Revelation 13:8).

Spreading Good News

Jesus went from town to town preaching that the kingdom of God is at hand and people should repent. Thus He send His desciples to preach the gospel in His name (Matthew 28:18-20).
